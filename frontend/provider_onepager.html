SSVF Health Care Navigator

Who is the SSVF Health Care Navigator?
A VA-funded community partner who helps Veterans access and coordinate care.
What is the SSVF Program?
The Supportive Services for Veteran Families (SSVF) Program is a VA-funded initiative that helps low-income Veterans and their families achieve housing stability. SSVF is delivered by local nonprofits in partnership with VA Medical Centers.
What does the Health Care Navigator do?
  • Helps Veterans enroll in VA health care and follow up on eligibility
  • Assists with scheduling and preparing for appointments (primary care, mental health, SUD, specialty care)
  • Promotes and supports VA Video Connect (telehealth)
  • Helps Veterans re-engage after ER/hospital visits
  • Addresses barriers: transportation, documentation, health literacy, technology
  • Links Veterans to community care when VA care is unavailable
Why might Navigators contact VA providers?
  • Coordinate care across VA and community systems
  • Clarify referrals or appointment status
  • Share observations about needs affecting treatment
  • Request participation in care planning (with Veteran consent)
SSVF Navigators are authorized collaborators, not outside case managers.
How can VA staff collaborate?
